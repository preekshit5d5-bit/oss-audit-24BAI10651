Every day, I rely on Vs code — a tool I did not buy,
but one that was gifted to me by a community of people
who believed that knowledge should be free.

To me, freedom means Freedom to run and modify.
That single word is why open source matters —
not just as a licensing model, but as a philosophy.
When source code is open, power shifts from corporations
to individuals. Anyone can read, learn, fix, and improve.

One day, I want to build Code and share it freely.
Not because I have to, but because someone once did the same for me.
That is the chain of open source — each generation
standing on the shoulders of the last.

Git taught me that even the most critical infrastructure
in the world can be built openly, maintained by volunteers,
and trusted by millions. That is not an accident.
That is what happens when freedom is taken seriously.

— Preekshit, 30 March 2026
